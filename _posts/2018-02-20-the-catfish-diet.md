Food in the field in Gabon was, quite frankly, nothing to write home about. Unless that was to complain about being tired of boiled catfish. Which I did. Several times. 

Limited budget (Gabon is a very expensive country as almost everything is imported), storage (no fridge) and cooking facilities (fire) meant that our field meals consisted of any combination of:
* Rice.
* Fish soup. Most often that was boiled catfish, sometimes with chillies or, if we were really lucky, tomatoes. Sometimes with eyes.
* Pasta.
* Sardines.
* Onions.
* Salt.
* Instant oats with sugar.

Although my boat pilot was a fabulous cook, there is only so much you can do with these ingredients, especially when you are looking at 2 meals a day for 3 people for 5 weeks. When we stayed in the village, we would occasionally get extra vegetables (aubergines, tomatoes), manioc or fruits (pineapple and bananas) from the plantation. Staying in the park rangers’ hut meant pretty much no fresh vegetables or fruits. As that was only the final 10 days, it was manageable, but meant I had a very long list of things I wanted to eat when I got home.

On a more positive note, I can definitely recommend the "catfish diet" as a good weight loss regimen: I did pretty much no exercise, spending all my days sitting in the boat or lounging around the camp, and I lost weight!
