Today I finally got to see the famous Taï Forest chimpanzees! 

Yesterday afternoon we finished our day survey early and together with Landry, our boat piolt, I headed into the forest to spend the night at the Chimp Camp. The camp is located 7km deep into the forest, and it takes about 1.5-2 hours to reach. This trek itself is already quite tiring, especially since this time we were carrying our sleeping kits, water and food. We reached the camp around 6pm, and had a well deseved rest, and a cuddle with the camp cat, before realising it's getting dark and we should think of having some dinner. After our recent plantation "raid" we are now flush with avocados, so I made a very non-Ivorian bread with gucacamole and sardines. I suspect Landry was missing his wife's cooking after that...

This morning we got up at 5am, and after a quick coffee headed out with the guide to reach the chimps' sleeping site. Walking through the forest in the dark, especially off the trail, is also not the easiest, and I regretted not bringing wellies after we had to cross several streams. Suddenly we heard the tell-tale screams and hoots of the chimpanzees: they were still up in the tress. We put on our face masks (to avoid passing any germs and accidentally wiping out the entire population) and waited for them to descent.

After about an hour of leaves, fruits and possible poo dropping all around us, the chimps finally started to come down onto the forest floor. First we followed the dominant male travelling with a few females and young, but he was not ready for the paparazzi and kept moving away from the camera. From time to time, we'd hear more hoots, screams and drumming - the chimp forest communication is a lot more conspicuous than my crocs! We joined the rest of the group, busy grooming, eating and smashing fruits open with rocks. As difficult as walking though the dense vegetation, sweating in 100% humidity and being covered in ants, mosquito and fly bites can be, it was totally worth it!
