One of the great benefits of our basecamp at the lodge, aside from the luxuries of electricity, my own bed and a bathroom, is internet reception. The signal is not great, cuts out a lot, and usually requires a bit of hunting: zig-zagging across the lodge garden, or a walk uphill to the car park. I often seem to get reception on the forest path leading uphill, so I will usually spend an hour or two loitering there and trying to catch up on my WhatsApps, email and news. This morning, I had company: a diana monkey, a hornbill, and a frog. Both the diana and the hornbill stayed around for a while, each making their respective calls, making me feel like we were all hunting for the best signal together. The frog stayed silent, but that's to be expected from a frog on a sunny morning.

In the afternoon the lodge suddenly became a hub of activity again: the staff arrived, followed by three tourists. They came fully kitted out in wellies, binoculars and large camera lenses, and I guessed they were birders before the staff confirmed it. It'll be a good opportunity for me to find out a bit more about the common birds we see here, but as we had a night survey to prepare for, I'll leave the bird conversation for another day.

Yesterday's rain flooded one of the rivers, which made our trip down away from the park a lot easier than yesterday. We got to (predictably, and fortunately) see more crocodiles, and on the way up we stumbled upon my first Western *Osteolaemus tetraspis*! I was pretty excited as I worried I wouldn't see these guys at all. Later on we also spotted a potto climbing through the canopy - to me they seem even more adorable than the slow loris, and I was so happy to finally see one in the wild!
