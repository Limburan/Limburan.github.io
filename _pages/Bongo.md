## **Where is Bongo River?**

Bongo forms the western border of Moukalaba Doudou National Park in south-western Gabon. It is connected to a few lakes: Logno Longo, Mafoumi, Ngore and Mouaga, and flows south into the large Ndongo Lagoon. The river, as well as the lakes and most of the lagoon, are surrounded by a rainforest, home to forest elephants, gorillas, chimpanzees, leopards, golden cats and hornbills.

## **What's so special about Bongo River?**

### **_Crocodiles_**

Three species of crocodiles can be found in the area: Nile crocodile *Crocodylus niloticus*, dwarf crocodile *Osteolaemus tetraspis*, and the Critically Endangered Central African slender-snouted crocodile *Mecistops cataphractus*. All three are protected by Gabonese law, and all can be seen relatively easily.
